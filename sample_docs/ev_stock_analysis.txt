Electric Vehicle Stock Analysis — Investor Research Note
=========================================================

MARKET OVERVIEW (2024)
-----------------------
The global EV market reached approximately $500B in market capitalization 
across pure-play and transitioning manufacturers. Key players:

TESLA (TSLA)
- Market cap: ~$700-800B (volatile)
- Revenue: $97.7B (FY2023)
- Profit margins under pressure from price cuts
- Carbon credit revenue: $1.79B (significant buffer)
- Risk: Increased competition from Chinese manufacturers

BYD (BYDDF)
- Overtook Tesla in global EV unit sales in Q4 2023
- Strong position in Chinese market (largest EV market globally)
- Vertically integrated: makes own batteries (Blade Battery)
- Lower margin but higher volume strategy

RIVIAN (RIVN)
- Commercial van partnership with Amazon (100,000 vans ordered)
- Production ramp challenges in 2023-2024
- High cash burn rate — needs capital markets access

LEGACY AUTOMAKERS
-----------------
General Motors (GM):
- Committed $35B to EV/AV through 2025
- Ultium platform as foundation
- Delayed several EV launches due to cost concerns

Ford (F):
- Model e division losing ~$3B annually during transition
- F-150 Lightning gaining commercial fleet traction
- Maintained ICE profitability funding EV transition

Volkswagen Group:
- ID series gaining European market share
- PowerCo battery subsidiary targeting €20B revenue by 2030
- Carbon compliance pressure in EU driving urgency

CARBON CREDIT MARKET DYNAMICS
-------------------------------
Under California's ZEV mandate and EU regulations:
- EV manufacturers earn credits for zero-emission vehicle sales
- These credits can be sold to ICE manufacturers who miss quotas
- Tesla's carbon credit windfall: total $9B+ since 2009
- As regulations tighten in 2025-2026, credit prices may rise

INVESTMENT THESIS
-----------------
Bull case for EV stocks under rising carbon taxes:
1. Operating cost advantage of EVs widens (electricity vs gasoline)
2. Government subsidy programs funded by carbon revenues
3. Regulatory pressure forces ICE-to-EV fleet transitions
4. Carbon credit income stream grows for pure-play EV makers

Bear case:
1. Higher energy costs affect EV manufacturing too
2. Consumer price sensitivity limits demand growth
3. Infrastructure gaps (charging networks) slow adoption
4. Chinese competition compresses margins globally

VALUATION METRICS (2024)
-------------------------
- EV market historically valued on revenue multiples (not P/E)
- Industry average: 3-8x forward revenue for growth-stage EV makers
- Profitability becoming more important as growth rates normalize
- Cash burn rate critical for pre-profit companies (Rivian, Lucid)
